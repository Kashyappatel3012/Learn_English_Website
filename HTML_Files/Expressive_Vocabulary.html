Expressive Vocabulary

Expressive vocabulary refers to the words that an individual actively uses when speaking or writing to communicate their thoughts and ideas. It is the set of words that a person can effectively employ in their own expression, distinguishing it from receptive vocabulary, which involves understanding words when reading or listening.

In language development, individuals aim to expand their expressive vocabulary to articulate their thoughts more precisely and communicate effectively with others. This involves not only knowing the meanings of words but also using them appropriately in various contexts.

The development of expressive vocabulary is crucial for effective communication in both spoken and written forms. As individuals learn and acquire new words, they gain the ability to convey their thoughts, feelings, and experiences with greater nuance and accuracy.

Assessments of expressive vocabulary are often used to evaluate a person's communication skills, especially in educational and clinical settings.
